# Blog Overview: *Add Parkinson's and Shake Well*

**URL:** `addparkinsonsandshakewell.wordpress.com`

---

## The Author — Mike Atkin

Mike is a **retired secondary school maths teacher** living in the London area (Old Bexley and Sidcup). He was diagnosed with **Parkinson's disease** and began the blog as a way to write about life with the condition. He is tall (6'3"), has a dry, self-deprecating British wit, a love of sport, music, and Doctor Who, and refers to his wife affectionately throughout as "my much better half."

He has Scottish heritage on his mother's side and is a fan of Runrig and the SNP. He was involved in National League Basketball in the 1980s (Team Talbot, Guildford) and has a background interest in jazz. His blog motto, which he coins in one post, is:

> *"Blog as if no-one else is ever gonna read this"*

---

## The Blog

Active roughly **2016–2021**, across ~100+ posts. The title is a pun on the medicine label instruction "add [medication] and shake well."

### Recurring Themes

| Theme | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Living with Parkinson's** | Comfy chairs being a trap, hotel accessibility disasters, yoga as the only man in the class, temperature sensitivity, the exhausting meds cycle ("Delayed Onset, Sudden Off") |
| **Medication & Side Effects** | Hallucinations (he invents a talking bear named after a jazz novel), compulsive behaviours, the DOSO effect, a brilliantly deadpan post arguing water is a miracle supplement (it is, of course, water) |
| **Politics & Current Affairs** | Sharp commentary on the 2017 election, Brexit, Boris Johnson, Dominic Cummings, Matt Hancock and COVID-19 policy — written from an informed, frustrated, but always humorous angle |
| **Teaching & Education** | Defends teachers with 2:2 degrees, critiques Ofsted, shares classroom anecdotes including a memorable Asperger's student who did *exactly* what was asked and nothing more |
| **Sport** | Andy Murray, Arsenal, football managers (Wenger, Mourinho, Ferguson), the Paralympics, the Ryder Cup, 1980s basketball |
| **Doctor Who** | An extended countdown series and multiple posts on the show |
| **Language & Grammar** | Pedantic-but-warm posts on "a" vs "an", Humpty Dumpty-ism (using words to mean whatever you want), political doublespeak |
| **Music** | Runrig, jazz, a multi-part "Recorder Experiment" series |

### Series Posts

- *Things I Dislike About Parkinson's* — numbered, ongoing
- *Humpty Dumpty-ism* — on language misuse (4+ parts)
- *Countdown to the New Dr Who Series* — daily countdown posts
- *Unbelievable* — political/social absurdity (3 parts)

## Tone & Style

Mike writes with **warm, dry British humour** — self-deprecating about his illness but never self-pitying. He weaves pop culture (Monty Python, Orwell, Doctor Who) into everyday observations, uses footnotes and asides naturally, and has a mathematician's love of precision applied to prose. His political writing is pointed but grounded in evidence (statistics, direct quotes). The overall feel is of a sharp, curious, funny man who is genuinely good company on the page.
